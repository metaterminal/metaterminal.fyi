On the third they found the carcass,<br/>
adrift in the high Arctic,<br/>
with herring gulls and frozen hulls<br/>
floating like a dream. <br/>
They cracked it open like a barrel<br/>
and found such things inside:<br/>
<i>Posel světla</i>, Love Labour’s Won—<br/>
the second half of Kubla Khan—<br/>
and most strange of all they found in there<br/>
A DISC<br/>
which was onyx-black, with gentle grooves,<br/>
a central hole rimmed fine in bronze.<br/>
They took it back onboard their ship.<br/>
Fed their cassette spools with tape,<br/>
made it run, however one does,<br/>
and touched the disc with golden hands<br/>
to give the wheel a spin.<br/>
This is what it spoke.<br/>

&emsp; <i>Testing? Testing? Are you there?<br/>
&emsp; I suppose you can’t respond. That’s fair.<br/>
&emsp; We come to you from a distant year<br/>
&emsp; Where we’ve discovered, it appears,<br/>
&emsp; A CYLINDER<br/>
&emsp; &emsp; &emsp; &emsp; &emsp; &emsp;&nbsp; of hand-span’s width<br/>
&emsp; An arm-bone tall, thickness a fifth.<br/>
&emsp; Material seems some sort of wax.<br/>
&emsp; If it had markings, now it lacks.<br/>
&emsp; But there are imprints on the sides<br/>
&emsp; Vibrations, maybe, now incised<br/>
&emsp; On physical, trapped amber-like.<br/>
&emsp; We’ve procured for ourselves a mic.<br/>
&emsp; Material damaged; very poor.<br/>
&emsp; No chance of any recovery, for<br/>
&emsp; Once the data’s lost, it’s lost.<br/>
&emsp; Time does that. Sunrays. Rain. Or frost.<br/>
&emsp; Provenance some southern plain<br/>
&emsp; A desert-place, where sand has claimed<br/>
&emsp; The wild horizon, encircling round.<br/>
&emsp; How then, now, to produce a sound?<br/>
&emsp; A metal needle’ll slice the face.<br/>
&emsp; So we use thread, bound to the base<br/>
&emsp; And with each spin the web vibrates.<br/>
&emsp; Herein records the sound it makes:</i>

&emsp; &emsp; For the emperor <br/>
&emsp; &emsp; &emsp; &emsp; recovered now<br/>
&emsp; &emsp; &emsp; &emsp; &emsp; &emsp; A SPOOL<br/>
&emsp; &emsp; &emsp; &emsp; &emsp; &emsp; &emsp; wound round<br/>
&emsp; &emsp; the oddest thing<br/>
&emsp; &emsp; &emsp; &emsp; white like bone<br/>
&emsp; &emsp; &emsp; &emsp; &emsp; &emsp; tighter than that.<br/>
&emsp; &emsp; &emsp; &emsp; &emsp; &emsp; &emsp; &emsp; the ribbon, then,<br/>
&emsp; &emsp; black, deep brown<br/>
&emsp; &emsp; &emsp; &emsp; rough to touch<br/>
&emsp; &emsp; &emsp; &emsp; &emsp; &emsp; runs between <br/>
&emsp; &emsp; &emsp; &emsp; &emsp; &emsp; &emsp; &emsp; another bone<br/>
&emsp; &emsp; and the whole thing<br/>
&emsp; &emsp; &emsp; &emsp; trapped in glass<br/>
&emsp; &emsp; &emsp; &emsp; &emsp; &emsp; square, on all sides<br/>
&emsp; &emsp; &emsp; &emsp; &emsp; &emsp; &emsp; &emsp; or a brick<br/>
&emsp; &emsp; it makes a noise:<br/>
<br/>
<br/>
&emsp; &emsp; &emsp; &emsp; &emsp; &emsp; &emsp; &emsp; or so we think.

&emsp; <i>More work to come. Now we sail north.<br/>
&emsp; Further research follows forth.</i>

The disc stops spinning; they halt the tape<br/>
and sail homewards along the cape.
